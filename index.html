High-uncertainty estimate. Disengagement is invisible by nature — it does not appear in headcount data, payroll records, or standard HR reporting. Default figures apply Gallup Australia benchmarks (18–20% active disengagement; 34% salary-equivalent productivity loss) to your cohort. The non-promotable task burden is modelled from McKinsey Women in the Workplace and AHRI research. These figures cannot be validated without direct organisational analysis. This tool surfaces the question — it does not answer it.
Combined estimate treats turnover and disengagement as additive. In practice they overlap — a disengaged employee is more likely to exit, and exit risk increases presenteeism among remaining peers. A formal audit would model these interactions using your data.

This diagnostic is a prompt for inquiry, not a verdict. Each flag should be treated as a hypothesis to test — not a confirmed finding.
